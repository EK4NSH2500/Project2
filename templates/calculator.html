Echo

Sound heard after reflection from a surface is called echo. Just like heat or light, when sound falls on a surface, it is partly reflected and partly absorbed. Sound like Light can bounce back from an object. When Sound hits a Solid Surface more than 17 Metres away from the Source, we can hear the Sound again.

Sound In Air

Air is almost an ideal Gas. The Formula for the Distance from the Source of Sound in Air is:

d = s*t/2

where:

  • d - the distance from the source of sound
  • t - time taken for the sound to travel
  • s - the speed of sound in air i.e. 340 m/s
